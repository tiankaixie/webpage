# Design Context

## Product
- A personal website for Tiankai Xie, presenting his identity, research, projects, writing, and cultural interests.
- The site should connect technical research with a broader experimental and philosophical practice.

## Users
- Potential research and creative collaborators evaluating Tiankai's perspective and body of work.
- Members of the public discovering his writing, projects, and interests.

## Core Use Cases
- Quickly understand who Tiankai is, what he works on, and how he sees the world.
- Read essays and technical writing without visual distraction.
- Explore selected research, software projects, and music references.
- Find a clear path to contact or follow Tiankai.

## Brand Tone
- Experimental and artistic rather than conventionally corporate or academic.
- Thoughtful, philosophical, precise, and quietly technical.
- Personal and authored; avoid the appearance of a generic portfolio template.

## Visual Direction
- Use a restrained monochrome foundation with deliberate experimental moments.
- Favor editorial composition, asymmetry, negative space, and strong typographic rhythm.
- Let motion and generative media support the ideas without competing with the writing.
- Preserve the current light/dark theme and minimal visual language while improving consistency.

## Interaction Principles
- Make identity and writing immediately accessible.
- Keep navigation direct and predictable even when visual composition is experimental.
- Use motion to communicate state or atmosphere, with reduced-motion support.
- Treat mobile layouts as intentionally composed views, not compressed desktop layouts.

## Constraints
- Maintain strong readability, keyboard accessibility, semantic structure, and responsive behavior.
- Keep Astro's static-first architecture and limit client-side JavaScript to genuinely interactive experiences.
- Preserve fast page loads and avoid decorative dependencies without a clear role.

## Avoid
- Generic portfolio cards, startup-style hero sections, and decorative dashboard patterns.
- Bright AI-associated gradients, excessive glow, glassmorphism, and ornamental effects.
- Monospace typography used everywhere without hierarchy or editorial purpose.
- Visual experiments that obscure content, navigation, focus states, or contrast.
